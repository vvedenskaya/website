About

At Mars College I built a live map of Bombay Beach and the college campus - one LAN, one community-run internet. The idea was simple: show how that shared network actually sits on the ground, not only in a dashboard.

A small pipeline reads UniFi and UISP controllers and places radios, access points, and clients on the map with their real coordinates. You get three basemaps (streets, satellite, and a drone orthophoto), every device and link in view, light animation to suggest traffic moving across links and around APs, and a time bar to scrub sampled snapshots of usage and client connectivity over shorter and longer windows. For the frontend, we used OpenLayers.

Because Google Maps did not show the layout of our off-grid community, we captured DJI drone photos and stitched them into a single orthophoto aligned with Google Maps.

For each client the panel shows IP, signal strength, the associated access point, uptime, and traffic. Access point positions were checked on site with GPS.
